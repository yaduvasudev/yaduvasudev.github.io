CS6845 Pseudorandomness

Lectures

  • Lecture 1: Jan 15, 8am [Notes]
    Administrative details about the course, introduction to derandomization-PIT, introduction to the probabilistic method.
  • Lecture 2: Jan 16, 12pm [Notes]
    First moment method - application for MAX-CUT, Markov's and Chebyshev's inequality, statement of Chernoff inequality.
  • Lecture 3: Jan 18, 11am [Notes]
    Chernoff's inequality, Hoeffding's extension and application to error reduction in randomized algorithms.
  • Lecture 4: Jan 19, 10am [Notes]
    Derandomization of the MAX-CUT algorithm - method of conditional expectations and pairwise independence.
  • Lecture 5: Jan 22, 8am [Notes]
    Construction of pairwise independence hash families, brief introduction to finite fields (Madhu Sudan's notes on finite fields).
  • Lecture 6: Jan 23, 10am [Notes]
    Randomness efficient error reduction with pairwise independent hash families, introduction to expanders and definition of vertex expansion.
  • Lecture 7: Jan 25, 11am [Notes]
    Error reduction using vertex expanders, eigenvalues of graphs, introduction to spectral graph theory.
  • Lecture 8: Jan 29, 8am [Notes]
    Definition of spectral expansion, spectral expansion implies mixing, random walks and undirected reachability.
  • Lecture 9: Jan 30, 12pm [Notes]
    Checking reachability in undirected graphs using random walks.
  • Lecture 10: Feb 1, 11am [Notes]
    Equivalence between notion of expansion, expander mixing lemma.
  • Lecture 11: Feb 2, 10am [Notes]
    Proof of expander mixing lemma, analyzing random walks on expander graphs.
  • Lecture 12: Feb 5, 8am [Notes]
    Random walks on expanders, introduction to product construction of explicit constant degree expanders.
  • Feb 6, 12pm: Tutorial session, discussion of solutions of assignment 1.
  • Lecture 13: Feb 8, 11am [Notes]
    Zig-zag product of graphs, fully explicit family of constant-degree expanders.
  • Lecture 14: Feb 9, 10am (Notes to come soon. Ref: Entropy waves, the zig-zag product and new constant-degree expanders by Reingold, Vadhan and Wigderson)
    Analysis of zig-zag product construction, outline of the logspace algorithm for undirected reachability.
  • Lecture 15: Feb 12, 8am (Notes to come soon. Ref: Pseudorandomness by Salil Vadhan)
    Introduction to randomness extractors, definition of min-entropy, definition of deterministic and seeded extractors.
  • Lecture 16: Feb 13, 12pm (Ref: Pseudorandomness by Salil Vadhan)
    Existence of seeded extractors using the probabilistic method, statement of the leftover hash lemma.
  • Lecture 17: Feb 15, 11am (Ref: Pseudorandomness by Salil Vadhan)
    Explicit extractors from pairwise independent hash family, proof of the leftover hash lemma, notion of dispersers, simulation of RP algorithms using dispersers.
  • Lecture 18: Feb 16, 10am (Ref: Pseudorandomness by Salil Vadhan)
    Simulation of RP algorithms using dispersers (contd.), extractors as graphs, explicit extractors from expander families.
  • Feb 19, 8am : Quiz 1
  • Lecture 19: Feb 20, 12pm (Ref: Algorithmic introduction to coding theory (Lectures 1 and 2) by Madhu Sudan)
    Introduction to coding theory, definition of a code, channel and statement of Shannon's theorem for noiseless channels and noisy channels.
  • Lecture 20: Feb 22, 11am (Ref: Algorithmic introduction to coding theory (Lectures 1 and 2) by Madhu Sudan)
    Proof of Shannon's theorem for binary symmetric channel, statement of the converse of Shannon's theorem.
  • Lecture 21: Feb 23, 10am (Ref: Algorithmic introduction to coding theory (Lectures 1 and 2) by Madhu Sudan)
    Proof of the converse of Shannon's theorem, vector spaces, introduction to linear codes
  • Feb 26, 8am: Tutorial session, discussion of solutions of assignment 2.
  • Lecture 22: Feb 27, 12pm [Notes]
    Generator matrix and parity check matrix of a linear code, Hamming codes, Hamming bound.
  • Lecture 23: Mar 1, 11am [Notes]
    Asymptotics of codes, Gilbert-Varshamov bound, expander codes.
  • Lecture 24: Mar 5, 8am [Notes]
    Expander codes (contd.), linear-time decoding algorithm, lossless expanders.
  • Lecture 25: Mar 6, 12pm [Notes]
    Singleton bound, Reed Solomon codes - definition, encoding, distance.
  • Lecture 26: Mar 8, 11am [Notes]
    Unique decoding of Reed Solomon codes: Welch-Berlekamp algorithm.
  • Lecture 27: Mar 9, 10am [Notes]
    List decodable codes, Johnson's bound, list decoding Reed Solomon codes up to \( 1-2\sqrt{R} \).
  • Lecture 28: Mar 12, 8am [Ref: Chapter 13, Section 13.2.3, Essential Coding Theory]
    Sudan's list decoding algorithm for Reed Solomon codes, outline of the Guruswamy-Sudan list decoding algorithm.
  • Lecture 29: Mar 13, 12pm [Ref: Chapter 13, Section 13.2.4, Essential Coding Theory for the Guruswamy-Sudan algorithm, these notes for topics related to the hardness of the permanent function]
    Guruswamy-Sudan list decoding algorithm: proof of correctness, average-case hardness of the permanent function-statement of Lipton's theorem about random self-reducibility.
  • Lecture 30: Mar 15, 11am [Notes]
    Lipton's proof of the random self-reducibility of the permanent, average case hardness of the permanent using the Welch-Berlekamp decoder-Gemmel and Sudan's proof.
    Ref: Highly resilient correctors for polynomials by Gemmel and Sudan.
  • Lecture 31: Mar 16, 10am [Notes]
    Application of list decoding - proof of the average case hardness of the permanent by Cai, Pavan and Sivakumar.
    Ref: On the hardness of Permanent by Cai, Pavan and Sivakumar.
  • Lecture 32: Mar 19, 8am [Ref: Survey by Luca Trevisan, Section 3]
    Local decoding, definition of a smooth decoder, local decoding of Walsh-Hadamard code.
  • Lecture 33: Mar 20, 12pm [Ref: Survey by Luca Trevisan, Section 3]
    Application of local decoder in hardness amplification, Reed Muller codes.
  • Lecture 34: Mar 22, 11am [Notes]
    Local decoding of Reed Muller codes, brief overview of using local decoding in private information retrieval.
  • Lecture 35: Mar 23, 10am
    Locally testable codes, local testing of Walsh-Hadamard code-description of the BLR test, introduction to Fourier analysis of Boolean functions.
    Ref: Chapter 1, Analysis of Boolean functions by Ryan O'Donnell.
  • Mar 26, 8am: Tutorial session, discussion of solutions of assignment 3.
  • Lecture 36: Mar 27, 12pm
    Fourier analysis of Boolean functions - inner products, uniqueness of the representation, Plancheral's identity, convolution.
    Ref: Chapter 1, Analysis of Boolean functions by Ryan O'Donnell.
  • Lecture 37: Apr 2, 8am
    Blum-Luby-Rubinfeld linearity tester-local testing of Walsh-Hadamard code, Application - weak statement of the PCP theorem.
    Ref: Chapter 1, Analysis of Boolean functions by Ryan O'Donnell.
  • April 3, 12pm: Quiz 2
  • Lecture 38: April 5, 11am
    Application of local decoding and testing of Walsh-Hadamard code - \( {\sf NP} \subseteq {\sf PCP}(\text{poly}(n),O(1)) \).
    Ref: Computational Complexity: A modern approach by Boaz Barak and Sanjeev Arora - Section 18.4.2
  • Lecture 39: April 6, 10am
    \( {\sf NP} \subseteq {\sf PCP}(\text{poly}(n),O(1)) \) - contd., introduction to locally list decodable codes.
    Ref: Some applications of coding theory in computational complexity by Luca Trevisan, Section 4
  • Lecture 40: April 9, 8am
    Local list decoding of Walsh-Hadamard codes - statement of the Goldreich-Levin theorem, application to hardcore predicates.
    Ref: Some applications of coding theory in computational complexity by Luca Trevisan, Section 4
  • Lecture 41: April 10, 12pm
    Application of Goldreich-Levin theorem - PAC learning of Boolean functions using Fourier analytic methods.
    Ref: Analysis of Boolean functions by Ryan O'Donnell, Chaptes 3 - Section 3.4
  • Lecture 42: April 12, 11am [Notes]
    Proof of Goldreich-Levin theorem using Fourier analytic methods.
    Ref: Analysis of Boolean functions by Ryan O'Donnell, Chaptes 3 - Section 3.4 & 3.5.
  • Lecture 43: April 13, 10am
    Introduction to small-bias spaces, connections with the Fourier spectrum, small-bias spaces from balanced codes.
  • Lecture 44: April 16, 8am
    Small-bias spaces from balanced codes - contd., explicit small-bias spaces using concatenated codes.
  • Lecture 45: April 17, 12pm [Notes]
    Cayley graphs - constructing expanding Cayley graphs from small-bias spaces, application of small-bias spaces - PRGs for branching programs.
  • Lecture 46: April 19, 11am
    Application of small-bias spaces - PRGs for branching programs (contd.)
  • Lecture 47: April 20, 10am
    Final lecture - review of the material covered in the course.
